Professional Summary

Zerihun Doda, PhD, is an Associate Professor of Sociology and Anthropology and a senior faculty member at Ethiopian Public Service University (EPSU), where he has served as Research Director since September 2021.

He holds a PhD in Anthropology and Biodiversity Management from the University of Kent, UK, and brings more than 28 years of experience in academia, research, curriculum design, consultancy, publication development, and advanced qualitative research training.

His work spans institutional reform, reorientation studies, public service delivery, qualitative methodology and CAQDAS, peace and conflict studies, sustainable development, social protection, cultural heritage, and community engagement.

Recent Strategic Leadership and Reform Contributions
  • Since November 2024, served as a member of the Ethiopian Civil Service University reorientation study national taskforce, serving as secretary of the national taskforce and editor in chief for the report productions, including production of policy brief; the reorientation study resulted in a drastic change to the course of the University, including its sourcing students from the 12th complete young students.
  • Served as a member of the national task force studying the reorientation of the Ethiopian Management Institute, which resulted in the production of a high level strategic report that provides long range roadmap to the future of the Institute.
  • Led the grand reform project for the University, for its Research, technology transfer and community engagement division, tasked with undertaking the AS-IS analysis of the University; defining the TO-BE and new organizational structure for the division; drafting the result and competency based job description; designing the competency assessment and redeployment framework; the reform work is ongoing and started in November 2025.
  • Since October 2024, served as a member of the PhD Program Design Consulting team in Transformation Leadership and Holistic Development and the journal establishment team for the International Journal of Transformational Leadership and Holistic Development for the Global Institute of Transformational Leadership; contributed to the overall program design for the PhD program; designed three courses, including Advanced Research Methods, Anthropology of Development, and PhD Dissertation Seminars; managed the development of the journal editorial policies; and led the journal review process from manuscript collection to the production of publishable issue for the journal.
Recent Teaching, Supervision, and Examination Highlights
  • Has been teaching Advanced Research Methodology courses and advanced qualitative data analysis software specializing on NVivo and Maxqda for PhD program students at Ethiopian Public Service University, Arba Minch University, Hawassa University, Addis Ababa University (Center for Human Rights), and Haramaya University.
  • Has supervised several PhD students at EPSU, Haramaya University, and Hawassa University and has served as external examiner for over dozens of PhD dissertations and several dozens of masters theses at various universities, including Addis Ababa University. Most recently, examined a PhD student for UNISA.
Community and Media Engagement
  • Provided professional and voluntary community services by engaging in media appearances four times in 2024-2025, including interviews with the Addis Media Network and the Finfine FM Service on issues including the utility of national reconciliation efforts, the impacts of graduation celebrations for children completing kindergarten schools, and the values of solidarity based social support during Ethiopian festive seasons.
